Title: Why Russia Is Exporting So Much Vaccine

Text: Listen and follow The Daily Apple Podcasts | Spotify | Stitcher In recent years, Russia has tried to reassert its global influence in many ways, from military action in Ukraine to meddling in U.S. elections. So when Russia developed a coronavirus vaccine, it prioritized exporting it to dozens of other countries, at the expense of its own people. A national tradition of vaccine expertise — and a ban, instituted under the Trump administration, on U.S. vaccine exports — made this an area where Russia could look to show up the West. Today, we look at how Russia has put vaccine diplomacy to work.
